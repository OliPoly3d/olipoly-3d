How it works

Start with the problem, not the printer.

You do not need a finished file or a perfect drawing. A photo, rough measurement, broken part, sketch, or short description is often enough to begin figuring out the right next step.

1Show the need

Send the photo, sketch, broken part, or rough idea.

2Design around it

The part is measured, modeled, and shaped for the actual use.

3Print and refine

Prototypes or finished pieces are checked for fit, function, and finish.

4Put it to work

You get a practical custom solution made for the real need.

Replacement parts

A good bottle did not need to be thrown away.

A missing or damaged part can make an otherwise useful product frustrating to use. When the exact replacement is hard to buy, a custom part can be designed around the actual object.

ThermoFlask replacement cap

Measured. Modeled. Printed. Tested.

This project shows how a practical replacement part can be created when the original component is missing, damaged, or no longer available.

Custom replacement part
1The problem

The bottle still worked, but the missing cap made it harder to use, store, and carry.

2The design

The opening, thread area, and fit were measured so the replacement could be built around the actual bottle.

3The printed part

The cap was printed and checked for fit, grip, and everyday function before calling it finished.

4The result

The bottle could stay in use with a practical custom replacement instead of being thrown away.

Measured from the real bottle
Designed for fit and handling
Printed as a usable part
Kept a product in service
