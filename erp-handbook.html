End-to-end workflows

The complete operating paths

Retail custom order: request → quote → payment → production → delivery

Capture the request

Customer uses Start Project or contacts you directly. If direct, enter the job in Production Control so it exists before quoting.

Production

Estimate production

Choose job type, quantity, printer, colors/materials, estimated grams, machine hours, due date, design/post-processing fees, and notes.

Cost preview

Check inventory and schedule

Use Hub Material Planner/Scheduler or Inventory Control. Do not quote a short lead time if material or printer capacity is risky.

Hub

Create and send quote

Use Quote Tool once cost and scope are clear. Retail quotes should show payment expectations and customer-friendly wording.

Quote

After acceptance, create/manage order

Accepted quote should become OP order. Orders Admin owns invoice, labels, tracker, status, and completion communication.

Orders

Print and close out

Complete production. Close job with actual grams/hours/scrap. Confirm inventory deduction and final margin.

Closeout

Record payment and close

Push/enter payment in Finance Pro. Mark order paid/closed. Send completion email if applicable.

Finance
Business / PO customer: request → quote → PO → invoice → Net terms

Start as business/PO job

Enter customer/company, requested item, batch quantity, due date, material, expected grams/hours, and any branded/client notes.

Production

Use business quote terms

Quote as Business / PO. Keep payment links hidden if customer pays through PO/Net terms. Include PO instructions clearly.

Quote

Wait for PO or written approval

Do not treat it as fully accepted until the PO or approval is received. Watch customer email/terms/tax-exempt details.

Customer action

Create/confirm OP order

Orders Admin becomes the owner. Add PO number, tax-exempt status, terms, invoice notes, and order status.

Orders

Generate PO invoice

Use invoice/PO invoice PDF. Confirm product and freight/shipping are separated when needed by customer accounting.

Invoice

Produce and close out

Close production with actual material/hours. Confirm inventory movement. Do not skip closeout on repeat/batch orders.

Closeout

Track AR

Record invoice/payment status in Finance Pro. Watch Net 30 aging from Hub Action Queue/Executive Dashboard.

Finance
Personal/free/gift project

Start in Production Control so it still counts against machine capacity and material. Set job type to Personal/Free and exclude from customer quote/payment unless it becomes a paid job. Decide whether to deduct inventory. If it uses meaningful material, deduct or log it so inventory stays honest.

Craft show / stock build

Start in Production Control as Craft Stock or Finished Goods Restock. Use Inventory Control for supplies and material. Finance Pro records booth fees, material purchases, card fees, and sales. Close jobs even when there is no customer so material and machine load stay accurate.

Repeat customer / reorder

Start in Hub Customer 360. Search the customer, review previous orders/materials/prices, then either duplicate the production assumptions manually or create a new Production Control job. Quote only if price/scope changed or the customer requires paperwork.

Customer paths

Retail vs PO vs internal work

Retail customer

Usually pays by card/PayPal/Venmo. Needs clear price, timeline, and tracker. Keep language simple and customer-facing.

  • Quote Tool: retail quote
  • Orders Admin: invoice/tracker
  • Finance Pro: sale/payment

Business / PO customer

May require tax exemption, PO number, freight split, Net terms, or invoice formatting. Do not show retail payment buttons if they use PO process.

  • Quote Tool: business/PO quote
  • Orders Admin: PO invoice
  • Finance Pro: AR/payment tracking

Internal / personal / test

Still belongs in Production Control if it uses time/material. Exclude from revenue but decide whether to deduct inventory.

  • Production: job record
  • Inventory: optional deduction
  • Finance: only if expenses/supplies matter
Production Control

How to run the shop queue

Production Control is the operational source for what must be printed, when, on which printer, with what material, and at what expected cost.

Watch for this: A job can look profitable before closeout but be wrong after actual material, reprints, or design time. Closeout is where the ERP becomes trustworthy.
Inventory Control

How to keep material honest

Inventory Control owns raw filament, non-filament supplies, free spools, reorder points, and movement history.

When material arrives

  1. Add raw filament or supply.
  2. Enter material/color/brand clearly.
  3. Enter cost and starting grams/quantity.
  4. Set reorder threshold if it matters.

When a job uses material

  1. Prefer production closeout deduction.
  2. Confirm actual grams and scrap.
  3. Check ledger if numbers look wrong.
  4. Avoid double-deducting.

When inventory looks wrong

  1. Check Data Health in Hub.
  2. Check movement ledger.
  3. Correct with adjustment notes.
  4. Do not guess without logging why.
Quote Tool

How to quote cleanly

Confirm production assumptions first

Do not quote until material, hours, quantity, and feasibility are understood.

Before Quote

Select customer/payment path

Retail quote should be easy to accept/pay. PO quote should support customer approval, tax exemption, PO number, and terms.

Terms

Check price logic

Confirm piece price, shipping/freight, deposit/balance, tax, discounts, and notes. Separate freight if customer accounting requires it.

Review

Send and follow up

Open quotes appear in Hub/Action Queue. Follow up if they age or block production planning.

Action Queue
Orders Admin

How to manage accepted work

Orders Admin is the owner once a quote is accepted or a PO/customer approval exists. It should answer: what did they buy, what do we owe them, what documents exist, what is the status, and did they pay?

Order setup

  • OP number present
  • Customer/contact correct
  • Quote/PO reference captured
  • Status and payment status set
  • Tax-exempt/terms set if needed

Documents

  • Invoice / PO invoice
  • Packing slip
  • Shipping label
  • Inner label
  • Manufacturing traveler

Close order

  • Production finished
  • Inventory deducted
  • Invoice/payment reconciled
  • Completion email sent
  • Status closed
Do not ignore: If Orders Admin ever loads with a white background or dead buttons, stop. That usually means a CSS/JS injection or script error broke the page. Check console and restore the last known good orders-admin file before applying more patches.
Finance Pro

How to keep money clean

Record revenue

Record sales/payments with customer/order reference whenever possible. Do not rely only on order status.

Record expenses

Materials, supplies, shipping, packaging, marketplace fees, maintenance, software, booth fees, and admin setup should be categorized.

Reconcile

Use Hub Data Health for paid order/no finance entry, invoice overdue, or payment without order warnings.

Hub modules

How to read the command center

Action Queue

Do the highest score first. It combines overdue work, low stock, unpaid invoices, quote follow-ups, customer action, and data issues.

Customer 360

Use before repeat orders. Search customer/company/email and review revenue, orders, quotes, jobs, and follow-up signals.

Material Planner

Use before promising dates or placing orders. It compares active demand against available material and thresholds.

Scheduler

Use to check printer capacity, conflicts, unassigned jobs, and next 7-day load.

Executive Dashboard

Owner view for revenue, open work, inventory risk, quote/order value, and machine health.

Reliability + Data Health

Use when something feels off. It helps distinguish empty data from failed sync and flags likely record problems.

Operating routine

Daily and weekly checklist

Daily opening routine
  1. Open Hub.
  2. Check System Health and confirm cloud loaded.
  3. Review Action Queue top items.
  4. Check Scheduler for today/next 7 days.
  5. Check Material Planner before starting prints.
  6. Open Production Control and update statuses.
  7. Open Orders Admin for unpaid/needs invoice/ready delivery.
Daily close routine
  1. Close out completed jobs with actual grams/hours/scrap.
  2. Confirm inventory deductions.
  3. Update order/tracker statuses.
  4. Record payments or expenses in Finance Pro.
  5. Check Data Health for new warnings.
  6. Export backups when meaningful data changed.
Weekly routine
  1. Review Executive Dashboard.
  2. Review open quotes and overdue invoices.
  3. Review low-stock and upcoming material needs.
  4. Check duplicate customers/material records.
  5. Review printer PM/machine hours.
  6. Save a repo backup and Supabase/export backup.
Troubleshooting

When something goes wrong

Page loads but data is empty
  1. Check Hub System Health.
  2. Confirm you are logged in.
  3. Hard refresh with Ctrl + Shift + R.
  4. Check if page says cloud failed vs truly empty.
  5. Look for recovery snapshot/backup export.
  6. Do not re-enter lots of data until you know whether it is a load issue.
Buttons do not work
  1. Open browser console with F12.
  2. Look for red JavaScript errors.
  3. If Orders Admin is affected, suspect a script/CSS regression first.
  4. Restore the last known good version of that page if needed.
  5. Do not keep applying patches over a broken page.
Inventory does not match reality
  1. Check movement ledger.
  2. Check whether job closeout deducted material twice or not at all.
  3. Check duplicate material/color/brand records in Data Health.
  4. Make a manual adjustment with a note rather than silently editing numbers.
Quote/order/customer linkage looks wrong
  1. Search Customer 360.
  2. Check quote number and OP order number.
  3. Check customer spelling/email/company consistency.
  4. Use Data Health to find orphan accepted quotes or orders without customer.
Payment status does not match Finance
  1. Check Orders Admin payment status.
  2. Check Finance Pro transaction entry.
  3. Confirm customer/order reference is present.
  4. Fix the source record and re-check Hub Data Health.
Mobile layout feels cramped

Use the page-specific mobile views and collapse sections when possible. Internal ERP pages are data-dense; do daily work from Hub first, then open the specific page only when you need to edit records.

Watch for this

Common mistakes to avoid

Quoting before checking material

Always check inventory and scheduler before promising dates or price on bigger jobs.

Skipping closeout

Closeout protects inventory, margin, and future estimates. Do not leave completed work open forever.

Mixing PO and retail terms

Business customers may need PO, Net 30, tax exemption, freight split, and no retail payment buttons.

Duplicate customer names

Use consistent customer/company/email naming so Customer 360 and Data Health work.

Manual edits without notes

If adjusting inventory or finance, add a note explaining why. Future you needs the audit trail.

Ignoring sync warnings

If cloud sync fails, stop and resolve before adding lots of new data.
